Top 5 Books

"Show me your books, and I'll show you your future;"

The Intelligent Investor
- Benjamin Graham

The Intelligent Investor was the first book I read about value investing. Written by Benjamin Graham, the mentor of Warren Buffett, it remains a cornerstone in the world of investment literature. The book emphasizes patience, discourages frequent trading, and provides timeless principles for building a robust investment strategy. Even 50 years after its publication, it’s still considered the "bible" of value investing. While theoretical in nature, its insights are invaluable for anyone embarking on an investment career.

The Lean Startup
- Eric Ries

A must-read for entrepreneurs and startup enthusiasts, The Lean Startup teaches the importance of testing your product with a prototype before committing resources. Eric Ries highlights the significance of understanding customer needs early in the process, as many customers may not even realize they need your product until they see it in action. This book is a practical guide to navigating the uncertainties of launching a business.

Linux Basics for Hackers

Perfect for beginners entering the field of cybersecurity, Linux Basics for Hackers introduces the essentials of Linux and its role in the tech world, including why it’s the operating system of choice for over 70% of servers globally. The book also provides an overview of networking and fundamental exploitation techniques, making it an ideal starting point for a cybersecurity career.

Black Hat Python
- Justin Seitz

Black Hat Python is a hands-on guide to learning Python for offensive security and penetration testing. Authored by Justin Seitz, this book takes you deep into the use of Python for automating hacking tasks, creating custom tools, and understanding how exploits work. It’s an essential read for cybersecurity professionals who want to elevate their skills and develop creative approaches to real-world challenges.

Thinking, Fast and Slow
- Daniel Kahneman

A groundbreaking exploration of the human mind, Thinking, Fast and Slow delves into the dual systems that drive our thoughts—intuition and logic. Authored by Nobel laureate Daniel Kahneman, this book uncovers the cognitive biases that shape our decisions and provides insights into improving our thinking processes. A must-read for anyone interested in psychology and decision-making.
